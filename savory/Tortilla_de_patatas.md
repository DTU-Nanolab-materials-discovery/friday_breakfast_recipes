# Tortilla de patatas (truita de patates)

## Ingredients (for 4 people)

- 3 big potatoes (palm of your hand kinda big)
- 4 eggs
- Salt
- Pepper
- Olive oil (do not even think about using butter)

## Tools
- Fork
- Small deep pan
- Small pot + lid
- Peeler
- Knife
- Bowl

## Instructions
1. Peel potatoes with the peeler.
2. Cut in slices approx half to 1 cm thick (with the knife).
3. Put the potato slices in the pot, add a generous amount to olive oil (enough to coat) - you can add more (at danger of having absolute oiliest omelette), this is to soften the potatoes.
4. Heat at low-medium heat until they are soft (you can cut them with the knife with almost no force).
5. Meanwhile, add eggs to bowl and whisk them (the wider the movements, the better, the fluffier). Add two turns (or small pinches) of salt and 1 of pepper per egg.
6. When potatoes are done and still hot, put them in the egg bowl and mix. This will start cooking the omelette.
7. Pour into small deep pan (oiled) and cook at medium-high heat until borders are cooked. 
8. Grab a plate or a lid or a special tool to turn omelettes and flip it.
9. Pour back (in the same plane, do not be a brute, slid it back) into the pan.
10. Wait until it looks set (this is experience and pan-based). Time depends on how liquid vs. done you like it. You can poke it with a stick to check if panicking.
11. Welcome to hispanity, you are now part of the local inquisition brigade.
